Make sure to put out any burning objects the right way. Unsafely discarded smoking materials can smolder unnoticed for days before catching fire. Never throw away your used tobacco on the ground or in plant pots.

While cooking, never leave the kitchen unattended. The time it takes for a fire to start is concise. Get someone to watch the food for you if you have to leave the kitchen for any reason at all, or simply turn everything off until you return.

Keep a blanket or fire extinguisher nearby at all times! Your best line of defense in the case of a fire starting will be blankets and fire extinguishers. To ensure that anything that does catch fire in your home can be extinguished as quickly as possible, everyone in the family should know how to use a fire extinguisher. Fire blankets are also excellent for extinguishing little fires before they get more extensive and more furious.

Keep your cables clean. Try to be considerate and check the wire for damage or chewing before plugging anything into a socket.Broken wires should be replaced as soon as possible because they can provide a risk for a fire.

Any combustible items should be stored safely. If exposed to a significant heat source, household cleansers and products like hair spray or shaving cream may become quite dangerous. Always keep these safely locked up and away from heaters in a cool location.
